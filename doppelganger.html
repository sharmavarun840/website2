The Science Behind Doppelgängers

Authored by: Indu Sharma (Ph.D.) and Varun Sharma (Ph.D.)

Do strangers who look exactly alike share more than just looks? A recent study says yes — they often share a surprising amount of genetic similarity.

Using AI to identify unrelated look-alikes and then analyzing their genomes, researchers found that they shared genetic variants influencing facial structure, height, and even behavioral traits.

Genetics Over Environment

Interestingly, facial resemblance was mostly genetic — environment played a lesser role. This supports the idea that DNA heavily influences how we appear.

Look-alikes even shared lifestyle traits like smoking and education, suggesting a genetic basis for behaviors too.
